# Sagip — Demo Guide

A ready-to-run script for showing Sagip live in about **3 minutes**. Everything below
uses the plain, non-technical language the app itself uses. Presenter notes (the
“why this matters” lines you can say out loud) are marked with 💬.

---

## The one-line story

> “Millions of Filipinos work far from home to support their families. Sagip makes sure
> that if something ever happens to them, the money they set aside reaches their family
> on its own — no lawyers, no paperwork, no waiting.”

---

## Before you start (2-minute setup)

1. The app is running and open in your browser.
2. Have **two things** ready side by side:
   - **Window A** — your account (the person setting money aside).
   - **Window B** — a second browser tab or your phone (the family member who receives).
3. That’s it. You don’t need to prepare any money — accounts start funded for the demo.

💬 *“I’ll play two people today: a mother working abroad, and her son back home.”*

---

## The demo, step by step

### 1. Create an account *(20 seconds)*
- Open the app and tap **Get started**.
- Enter a name (e.g. *Elena*), any mobile number, and a 6-number PIN.
- You’ll see a short welcome walkthrough. Tap through it, or **Skip**.

💬 *“Signing up takes a phone number and a PIN. No apps to install, nothing technical.”*

### 2. Add the loved one *(15 seconds)*
- On the home screen, tap the big **＋ Set aside** button at the bottom.
- Tap **Add someone new**, enter their name (e.g. *Mark*) and relationship (*Son*), then **Save**.

### 3. Set money aside *(20 seconds)*
- Give it a short name: **Monthly support**.
- Enter an amount: **15000**.
- For **“How often will you check in?”**, choose **Every minute** — this is the
  demo option that lets us see the whole story in one sitting.
- Tap **Set aside money**.

💬 *“She’s set aside 15,000 for her son. As long as she checks in, it stays completely
hers. Watch the ring — that’s the countdown until it opens to her family.”*

### 4. Check in *(10 seconds)*
- On the safety net screen, tap **“I’m okay — check in.”**
- Point out the ring resetting.

💬 *“One tap. That resets the timer and keeps the money in her hands. This is a real,
recorded action — it’s not a mock-up.”*

### 5. Share the link with family *(10 seconds)*
- Copy the **share link** and open it in **Window B** (the family member’s view).
- It says the money **isn’t open yet**, and shows the date it would open.

💬 *“Her son has the link. Right now it’s locked — he can’t touch it while she’s checking in.”*

### 6. The moment it matters *(wait ~1–2 minutes)*
- Now **don’t** check in. Let the timer run down.
- On the mother’s screen, the ring fills and turns gold: **“Open to family.”**
- On the son’s screen (Window B), it flips on its own to **Receive**.

💬 *“Say something happened — she couldn’t check in. The money simply opens to her son.
No one had to approve it. It just works.”*

### 7. The family receives *(10 seconds)*
- In Window B, tap **Receive**.
- Show the success screen: **All received**.

💬 *“One tap, and the money is his. That’s the whole promise of Sagip — peace of mind
for the person working abroad, and a safety net for the family they’re working for.”*

---

## If you want to show “I’m always in control”

Instead of letting the timer run out, tap **Take back** on the safety net screen. The
money returns to the mother instantly, and the son’s link shows **No longer available**.

💬 *“And if she changes her mind, she takes it back in one tap. Nothing ever happens
without her.”*

---

## Tips for a smooth demo

- **Use “Every minute.”** It’s built for exactly this. In real life people would
  pick weekly, monthly, and so on.
- **Open the family link in a separate tab or your phone** before the timer runs out, so
  the automatic “now open” flip is visible.
- If the screen doesn’t flip immediately, wait a few seconds — it checks on its own, and
  refreshing the page always works.
- Keep the language human. Never say “balance,” “claim,” “wallet,” or “transaction” — the
  audience should feel how simple it is.

## The 30-second pitch (for judges / VCs)

> Sagip is a safety net for the savings of people who work far from home. You set money
> aside for a loved one and check in now and then to keep it. If you ever can’t, it opens
> to your family automatically — they just tap a link to receive it. It’s built so that no
> single company or person decides when the money moves; the rules are set the moment you
> create it and can’t be broken. Simple enough for a grandparent to use, and it solves one
> of the most real, emotional problems in a country built on overseas work.

## Optional add-on: succession (~2 minutes)

Use this when a judge asks *“What if the receiver also dies?”*

1. When creating a safety net, enable **Add a backup person** and pick a sibling or spouse.
2. Run the normal demo through family **Receive**.
3. Show the receiver’s screen: **Received — guarded** — they must check in too.
4. Let the receiver’s timer lapse (use **Every minute** for their check-in interval as well).
5. Open the **backup’s claim link** — they receive next, with no staff intervention.

💬 *“The same rule that protected the OFW now protects the family — one layer deeper,
still enforced automatically, still no lawyers.”*

Then open **Admin** → click any transaction hash → show the live Stellar explorer record.

💬 *“Every step is a real on-chain transaction — not our database deciding anything.”*

---

## Optional add-on: monthly use (~30 seconds)

From **Family tools**, tap **Send a gift** → create an immediate or scheduled gift.

💬 *“Families use Sagip every month for birthdays and padala — not only for the worst day.
The same rails protect them when the worst day comes.”*
